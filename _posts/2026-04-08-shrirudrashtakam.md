## 🕉️ Verse 1: The Sky of Consciousness

**Sanskrit:**
```
> नमामीशमीशान निर्वाणरूपं विभुं व्यापकं ब्रह्मवेदस्वरूपम् ।
> निजं निर्गुणं निर्विकल्पं निरीहं चिदाकाशमाकाशवासं भजेऽहम् ॥१॥

**Transliteration:**
*Namāmīśamīśāna nirvāṇarūpaṃ vibhuṃ vyāpakaṃ brahmavedasvarūpam |*
*Nijaṃ nirguṇaṃ nirvikalpaṃ nirīhaṃ cidākāśamākāśavāsaṃ bhaje'ham ||1||*
```
**English Meaning:**
I bow to the Lord, the Ruler of the Universe, whose form is Liberation itself. He is the all-pervading Brahman, the embodiment of the Vedas. He is established in His own Self, beyond attributes, beyond doubt, and beyond all desire. He is the sky of consciousness, and the sky itself is His garment.

## 🔱 Verse 2: The Root of Omkara

**Sanskrit:**
```
> निराकारमोङ्कारमूलं तुरीयं गिराज्ञानगोतीतमीशं गिरीशम् ।
> करालं महाकालकालं कृपालं गुणागारसंसारपारं नतोऽहम् ॥२॥

**Transliteration:**
*Nirākāramoṅkāramūlaṃ turīyaṃ girājñānagotītamīśaṃ girīśam |*
*Karālaṃ mahākālakālaṃ kṛpālaṃ guṇāgārasaṃsārapāraṃ nato'ham ||2||*
```
**English Meaning:**
I bow to the Formless One, the root of Omkara, the Fourth State (Turiya). He is beyond speech, knowledge, and the senses. He is the Lord of the mountains, the terrible one who is the Death of Death itself (Mahakala), yet most compassionate. He is the abode of all virtues and the one who helps us cross the ocean of worldly existence.

---

## 🌌 Spiritual Insight
In these opening verses, the poet transitions from describing Shiva as the **Nirguna** (the formless, attributeless reality) to **Saguna** (the protector with form). He is "Stillness" in his absolute state and "Movement" in his cosmic dance.
